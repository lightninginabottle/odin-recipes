Paneer Butter Masala

Description

This is perhaps a premium north Indian or Punjabi curry, if not the king of the paneer curries. It is known for its mild sweet and spicy taste along with the goodness of fresh cream and butter topped off with moist paneer. It is one of the most widely eaten curries for a lot of vegetarians and is prepared for lunch or dinner and is served with freshly baked roti, chapati, naan or even rice.

Ingredients

Masala Paste Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 5 cloves
  • 2 pods cardamom
  • 2 onion (sliced)
  • 2 clove garlic
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 3 tomato (sliced)
  • 15 cashew

Curry Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 chilli
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • ¾ tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp cumin powder
  • ½ garam masala
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cream
  • 16 cubes paneer
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi
  • 2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

Preparation Steps

Instructions for the Gravy Base

  1. Firstly, in a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil and 2 tbsp butter
  2. Add 5 cloves, 2 pods cardamom and saute on low flame until it turns aromatic
  3. Now add 2 onion, 2 clove garlic, 1 inch ginger
  4. Saute until onions shrink slightly
  5. Then add 3 tomato, 15 cashew and saute for 2 minutes
  6. Cover and boil for 10 minutes, or until the tomatoes soften
  7. Cool completely and transfer to the blender and blend to a smooth paste
  8. Pass the curry to a filter to get rid of the seeds and skin
  9. Smooth and silky, your tomato-onion paste is ready. Now keep this aside

Instructions for Paneer Butter Masala

  1. Firstly, in a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil, 2 tbsp butter, 1 bay leaf and 1 chilli. Saute until the spices turn aromatic
  2. Now add 1 onion and saute until onions turn and golden brown
  3. While keeping the flame on low, add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, ¾ tsp coriander powder, ¼ tsp cumin powder, ½ garam masala and 1 tsp salt
  4. Saute until the spices turn aromatic. Ensure not to burn the spices
  5. Add in the prepared tomato-onion paste and mix well
  6. Cook until the oil separates from the masala paste
  7. Now add 1 cup water and mix well adjusting the consistency
  8. Then add in 2 tbsp cream and mix well
  9. Now you can add the 16 cubes of paneer and mix gently
  10. Simmer for 5 minutes, or until the paneer absorbs the flavour
  11. Now add 1 tsp kasuri methi and 2 tbsp coriander. Mix well

You are now ready to enjoy your delicious paneer butter masala with roti/naan/rice!
